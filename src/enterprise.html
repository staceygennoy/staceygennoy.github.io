Capital One Site Navigation Redesign

Capitalone.com has about 100 million visits a month. I was brought on to the team to redesign the navigation for two reasons: to improve the customer experience and to increase revenue for the business. There are three main areas that were improved: the content strategy, the customer experience and an improvement in brand recognition.

Summary:

  • UX lead
  • Interaction designer
  • Rapid prototyping and wireframe design
  • Facilitated workshops and meetings
  • Responsive Web Design (RWD)
  • Usability Testing
  • Collaboration with other designers, product owners and engineers

Success Metrics:

  • Contributed to a gain in “visitor to lead” conversion through a product architecture web redesign
  • 30% escalation in customer engagement through frequency of visits, content engagement and brand perception
  • Achieved annual revenue goals 6 months after deployment

Responsive web framework: Live on Capitalone.com

The Problem:

  • Navigation required a high cognitive load on the customer
  • Previous version was dated and difficult to use
  • Low-performing (levels of conversion compared to other pages)
  • Lack of user engagement
  • Unable to track users
  • Difficult to update

Before Design Update:

Process:

  • Conducted a site audit to discover several pain points and areas of fragmentation
  • Created personas, empathy maps and customer journey maps
  • Partnered with the content strategy team
  • Collaborated and discussed business needs with product partners to prioritize projects
  • Rapid creation of prototypes for A/B tests and experimentation
